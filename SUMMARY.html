System Architecture & Design Summary

1. Objective and Trade-offs

The goal was to transmit real-time audio frames (160 bytes, 20ms intervals) over a hostile UDP network while keeping deadline misses ≤ 1.0% and bandwidth overhead ≤ 2.0x. Because ARQ (retransmissions) require full round-trips that introduce unacceptable latency spikes, the architecture relies exclusively on Forward Error Correction (FEC).

2. Wire Protocol Design

To respect the bandwidth cap, we cannot simply duplicate every packet. Instead, we use an interleaved XOR parity scheme:

  • Sequence Compression: The 32-bit sequence number is downcast to an 8-bit wrap-around integer on the wire to save bytes, reconstructed at the receiver.
  • Parity Injection: On every even sequence number, the sender calculates the XOR parity of the two previous payloads (P_i-1 ⊕ P_i-2) and appends it to the packet.
  • Overhead: This yields an average overhead of ~1.5x, safely below the 2.0x limit.

3. Receiver & Jitter Buffer

The receiver utilizes a static 65536-element ring buffer (has_fec_buf and payload_buf) to handle extreme out-of-order delivery without dynamic memory allocation.

Upon receiving a packet, the receiver immediately pushes the current frame to the playout harness. It then sweeps the recent history window. If a packet was dropped, but the subsequent parity packet and one of the adjacent payloads exist, the missing payload is mathematically reconstructed (e.g., P_missing = Parity ⊕ P_known) and played before its deadline.
